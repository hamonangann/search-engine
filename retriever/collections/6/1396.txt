It is accepted as a position of international criminal law that head of state immunity does not apply before international tribunals [1] . Any such immunity that Kenya had was waived by them joining the ICC, which they did voluntarily.  Even so, just because someone has a position of power does not mean they should have impunity from liability for very serious crimes.
